OpenSplice DDS

OpenSplice DDS is a very high-performance, real-time, data-centric publish/subscribe middleware platform. It uniquely combines data-centric and real-time publish/subscribe features to provide next generation mission and business critical applications with the ideal platform for achieving high performance data distribution and management.

OpenSplice DDS complies with the full profile of the “OMG Data Distribution Service (DDS) for Real-Time Systems v1.2 Specification”, as it provides a complete implementation of both the “Data Centric Publish/Subscribe” (DCPS) Layer, and of the “Data Local Reconstruction Layer” (DLRL) Layer. This sets OpenSplice DDS as the leading OMG DDS-compliant COTS product available on the market.

OpenSplice DDS has been developed and optimized over years of research and development along with deployments in the most demanding mission and business critical distributed applications, such as, Combat Management Systems (CMS), Air Traffic Management/Control (ATM/C), Transportation, Telecommunications and Financial Services.

OpenSplice DDS provides integration with key technologies for expanding its applicability and easing its integration into your existing applications. Specifically, OpenSplice DDS has integration technologies with DBMS, such as Oracle, ANTS, MySQL, etc., Web Services, Real-Time Java(TM), and CORBA. Moreover, to boost your productivity OpenSplice DDS now comes with Model Driven Development (MDD) Power Tools, which guide you from the design of your system, to the tuning up to its final deployment.

Key Features:

  • Most complete 2nd generation OMG-DDS implementation supporting all DDS profiles
  • Field proven during a decade of deployment in mission critical environments
  • Targeting both real-time embedded & large-scale fault-tolerant systems
  • Highly optimized implementation from DDS-users for DDS-users
  • Total lifecycle support by graphical modeling up to remote maintenance
  • Support for network partitions, which are characterized by multicast group dynamically mapped on logical DDS partitions.
  • Support for multiple network interfaces, allowing transparent forwarding of DDS-data and or fault-tolerant communication paths between nodes.
  • Support realtime network-scheduler that schedules traffic based upon DDS QoS-policies like 'latency-budget' and 'transport priority'.

What's new

New in this release:

  • The priorities and scheduling clasess of the Durability service and splicedaemon threads can now be configured. Use OpenSplice DDS Configurator for explanation.
